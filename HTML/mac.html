Macaroni and Cheese

Cuisine: Italian

Creamy, cheesy, and utterly delicious, this recipe for Mac and Cheese is the ultimate in comfort food. Macaroni and cheese (also called mac and cheese in Canada and the United States and macaroni cheese in the United Kingdom) is a dish of macaroni and a cheese sauce, most commonly cheddar sauce. Its origins trace back to cheese and pasta casseroles dating to the 14th century in Italy and medieval England.Made with a gooey, buttery homemade cheese sauce, this stovetop macaroni and cheese recipe is one of the best mac and cheese you will ever make.

Ingredients

  • 16 oz elbow macaroni, cooked
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/3 cup all purpose flour
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 4 cups sharp cheddar cheese
  • 2 cups Gruyere cheese shredded
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 1/4 tsp smoked paprika

Instructions:

Preheat your oven to 350Â°F and lightly grease a spacious baking dish. In a bowl, combine a variety of shredded cheeses, setting aside. Cook the pasta until just shy of al dente, drain, and coat with olive oil. For the cheese sauce, melt butter, whisk in flour, and gradually add milk and cream until smooth. Stir in 4 cups of cheese until creamy, then combine with the pasta. Transfer half to the baking dish, layer with remaining cheese and pasta. Create a topping with panko, Parmesan, melted butter, and paprika, sprinkle it over, and bake for 30 minutes until golden. Serve this delicious mac and cheese promptly for a comforting meal.
