Classic Cheese Pizza

Cuisine: Italian

Sometimes all you want at the end of the day is a simple cheese pizza. This recipe turns simple into sublime with the addition of an exceptional pizza dough, low-moisture mozzarella cheese, and an easy to make tomato sauce that hits all of the right sweet and savory notes to marry all of the flavors in this pie. A simple garnish of fresh herbs, and you've got perfection on a plate.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4-oz. packet active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 c. warm water
  • 4 c. all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/4 tsp. kosher salt
  • 2 tbsp. olive oil, divided, plus more for brushing
  • 1 c. Homemade Pizza Sauce, divided
  • 1 c. cubed fresh mozzarella cheese, divided
  • 3 c. shredded low-moisture mozzarella cheese, divided
  • Fresh basil, to serve

Instructions:

In the initial pizza dough preparation, yeast, sugar, and warm water are combined, forming a dough mixed with flour, olive oil, and salt. After rising for 1.5 hours, the dough is divided, shaped into rounds, and left to rise. Preheat the oven with a pizza stone. Shape each dough round, transfer to a foil-covered sheet, and assemble with parmesan, pizza sauce, and mozzarella. Bake on the preheated stone, then brush with olive oil and top with basil leaves. Repeat for the remaining dough, emphasizing assembling just before baking for freshness.
