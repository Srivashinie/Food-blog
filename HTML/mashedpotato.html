Mashed Potatoes

Cuisine: Western cuisine

Mashed potato or mashed potatoes , colloquially known as mash is a dish made by mashing boiled or steamed potatoes, usually with added milk, butter, salt and pepper. It is generally served as a side dish to meat or vegetables. Roughly mashed potatoes are sometimes called smashed potatoes.

Ingredients

  • 5 lb. russet or Yukon Gold potatoes
  • 3/4 c. butter, plus more for topping
  • 1 (8-oz.) package cream cheese, softened
  • 1/2 to 3/4 cup half-and-half
  • 1/2 to 1 tsp. Seasoned Salt
  • 1/2 to 1 tsp. black pepper

Instructions:

Boil and mash the potatoes to perfection in this simple and comforting mashed potato recipe. Begin by peeling and cutting the potatoes into uniform pieces, cooking them until a fork effortlessly slides through. Drain the potatoes, return them to the pot, and mash over low heat, allowing steam to escape.


Incorporate butter, cream cheese, half-and-half, Seasoned Salt, and black pepper, stirring to create a creamy blend. Thaw them in the fridge overnight or reheat them from-frozen in the slow cooker, on the stove, or in the oven.
