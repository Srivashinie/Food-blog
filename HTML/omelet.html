French Rolled Omelet

Cuisine: French

A true French omelette, or omelet, is just eggs and butter, no filling. The egg is folded for a soft, tender texture. It's 10% ingredients and 90% technique, so it does take a bit of practice to perfect.A French omelette, is a tidy package of finesse and delicacy. Its exterior is smooth as silk, its inside moist and creamy, a sheet of tender egg cradling a filling of those very same eggs, softly scrambled.

Ingredients

  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh herbs, such as chives, tarragon, and flat-leaf parsley
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon unslated butter, plus more for serving
  • 2 tablespoons shredded Gruyère or cheddar cheese

Instructions:

To prepare this delightful omelet, crack eggs into a bowl, adding herbs, and seasoning with a light touch of salt and pepper. With wooden chopsticks, beat the mixture until well blended. Heat an 8-inch nonstick or seasoned carbon steel pan over high heat, adding butter and swirling it until foamy.


Pour the beaten eggs into the pan and simultaneously swirl it clockwise while vigorously stirring the eggs counterclockwise with chopsticks. This technique ensures small and creamy curds. Once a soft scramble forms, stop stirring, and use chopsticks to turn wispy edges into scrambled eggs. As a thin sheet of cooked egg forms on the pan's surface, promptly sprinkle cheese if desired.


Tilt the pan away, roll the egg crêpe filled with soft scrambled eggs, and tuck the edge into the omelet using chopsticks. Finally, turn out the omelet onto a plate, seam side down. Enjoy the creamy and flavorful result of this expertly crafted omelet.
